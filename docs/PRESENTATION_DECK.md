### Visual Layout

### Slide Content

**Section label:** THE FULL PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT LOOP

- Generate specs, user flows, and edge cases from a paragraph

**Stage 2: Design**
Icon: layout/pen tool
- Describe the experience — AI generates structure and components
- Iterate in conversation, not in Figma revisions

**Stage 3: Build**
Icon: code brackets
- AI writes boilerplate, logic, APIs, and tests
- You review, correct, and direct — 10× faster than writing alone

**Stage 4: Refine**
Icon: loop/refresh
- AI catches bugs, suggests optimizations, rewrites unclear code
- Ship, measure, describe the next iteration — repeat

**Bottom bar text:**
"AI is not a tool for one phase. It's a collaborator for the entire lifecycle."

---

### Speaker Notes
> "Here's the bigger picture — and this is the slide I want you to remember if you remember nothing else.
>
> AI doesn't just help you write code faster. It participates at every stage of building a product. From the first fuzzy idea to a deployed, tested, production system.
>
> Conceptualize: before you write anything, you can describe your idea to AI and have it challenge your assumptions, fill in the gaps, and generate a structured spec. This alone saves you from building the wrong thing.
>
> Design: you describe the user journey, the emotion you want, the flow — AI gives you back component structures, interaction states, edge case handling. No wireframe tool required.
>
> Build: this is the part people imagine when they hear 'AI coding.' But it's not just autocomplete. It's architecture suggestions, API design, database schemas, E2E test generation — entire systems from a description.
>
> Refine: once something is live, AI helps you iterate. You describe what's broken, what feels off, what you want to improve. It's a continuous loop.
>
> We're going to walk through all four of these using OrgBlueprint as the live example."

## SLIDE 4 — The Beauty of Vibe Design

### Visual Layout
3-column infographic. Left column slightly wider, center and right equal. Soft gradient background. Each column: large icon, bold headline, paragraph text. Optional: a thin decorative UI mockup floated on the right side.

### Slide Content

**Section label:** WHAT VIBE DESIGN ACTUALLY MEANS

**Column 1**
Icon: paint/intent arrow
**Intent-Driven UI**
Instead of placing components manually, you describe the experience: "This should feel like a conversation with a senior consultant — warm, confident, not robotic." AI translates emotional intent into structure, layout, and interaction patterns.

**Column 2**
Icon: brand/palette
**Consistency Without a Style Guide**
AI maintains visual and tonal consistency across every component — because you gave it the reference point once. The dark dashboard, the wizard flow, the chat widget — all coherent because the intent was stated once and applied everywhere.

**Column 3**
Icon: heart/connection
**Emotional Connection by Design**
The best products make users feel something. Vibe design starts there — with the feeling — and works backward to the interface. The result is a product that doesn't just function. It resonates.

**Pull quote (bottom, centered, italic):**
"You don't design the layout. You design the feeling. AI builds the layout."

---

### Speaker Notes
> "I want to spend a moment on vibe design specifically, because it's the less-discussed half of this shift and I think it's actually the more powerful one for non-developers in this room.
>
> Intent-driven UI means you stop starting with a blank canvas and a list of components. You start with a sentence. 'I want this to feel like a conversation with a trusted advisor.' That sentence shapes everything — the typography, the spacing, the tone of the microcopy, the animation. AI takes that intent and turns it into structure.
>
> Consistency without a style guide is something that's hard to achieve even on big teams. When I built OrgBlueprint, I didn't write a 20-page design system. I described the feeling once — dark, professional, not corporate — and AI applied it consistently across the entire product. The wizard, the dashboard, the floating chat widget, the PDF export — all coherent.
>
> And emotional connection. The best products aren't just functional. They make you feel something when you use them. OrgBlueprint was designed to feel like you're talking to a smart consultant who happens to work instantly. That emotional brief shaped every design decision. AI made that brief concrete.
>
> You don't need Figma. You need clarity about how you want people to feel."

## SLIDE 6 — The Problem: Traditional Planning is Broken

### Visual Layout
3-column infographic. Dark cards, each with an icon, a bold single-word headline (the pain), and 2–3 lines of context. A thin horizontal bar above: "The status quo in Salesforce implementation planning."

### Slide Content

**Section label:** THE PROBLEM WE'RE SOLVING

**Column 1**
Icon: clock / hourglass
**Slow**
A typical Salesforce scoping engagement takes 2–6 weeks before a single line is built. Multiple discovery calls. Multiple stakeholder reviews. Multiple drafts of a document no one will fully read.

**Column 2**
Icon: dollar / price tag
**Expensive**
Pre-sales and discovery work is either billed to the customer at consultant rates, or absorbed by the vendor as cost of sale. Either way, it's expensive — and often leads to the same templated output.

**Column 3**
Icon: broken loop / chaos
**Inconsistent**
Output quality depends on who runs the discovery. The same business need described to two different solution architects produces two very different blueprints. There's no systematic, repeatable way to get a good answer fast.

**Bottom bar (accent text):**
"The information to answer 'what do we need?' already exists. The bottleneck is time and access."

---

### Speaker Notes
> "Here's the problem OrgBlueprint was built to solve.
>
> When a company wants to implement Salesforce — or evaluate whether it's right for them — they go through a discovery process. Multiple calls. Multiple stakeholders. A solutions architect who asks the same 20 questions they've asked 100 times. And 4 weeks later, a document that says: 'you probably need Sales Cloud and Service Cloud.'
>
> That process is slow. It can take weeks before anyone writes a line of code or configures anything. That delay costs deals.
>
> It's expensive. Discovery time is consultant time — billed either to the customer or absorbed as cost of sale by the vendor. Neither is good.
>
> And it's inconsistent. The output depends entirely on who runs the discovery. Two equally qualified architects will give you two different recommendations for the same scenario.
>
> The information to answer these questions already exists — in the customer's needs, in Salesforce's product catalog, in implementation patterns that repeat across thousands of customers. The bottleneck is time and human bandwidth.
>
> OrgBlueprint eliminates that bottleneck."

## Q&A CHEAT SHEET

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| How long did it take to build? | Working prototype: one weekend. Production-quality with auth, tests, and deployment: ~2–3 more sessions. |
| What AI tools did you use? | Claude Code (coding agent with full codebase context), Gemini 2.0 Flash (conversation + codegen), Anthropic Claude (blueprint generation). |
| Do you need to know how to code? | Not deeply. But you need to know what correct looks like — so you can catch what's wrong. That judgment comes fast when you start building. |
| What if AI generates wrong code? | It will. Frequently. You review it. Correcting AI output is the job now — not writing every line. |
| Is it safe to use AI for production apps? | Review everything. Never paste API keys or secrets into prompts. Use environment variables. Don't skip security review. |
| Can non-developers do this? | Yes. But they need to develop judgment about what good output looks like. Start building something small — that judgment comes fast. |
